## Market environment

May opened with the VIX term structure in healthy contango and realized volatility sitting below its trailing-quarter median, a regime favourable to the systematic short-volatility activity that anchors Layer 2. Rates markets stayed range-bound through the reporting period; no single macro release dominated the tape. The month contained one scheduled CPI release and one FOMC meeting, both of which passed without the kind of cross-asset repricing that would have triggered Layer 3 episodic activity.

## Strategy activity

This being the inception month of the Composite, activity was deliberately measured: the intent for month one was to prove the operational stack — daily data pull, reconciliation, reporting — rather than to push sizing.

- **Layer 1 (capital base).** The 50/50 SGOV/USMV allocation was established at inception and held through the month; no rebalancing trigger fired. Contribution to total return was modest and consistent with a low-volatility ERP sleeve.
- **Layer 2 (systematic VRP).** A small number of 45-DTE short-put positions were opened on broad-index ETFs within the policy delta band. Entries were confirmed against the term-structure and IV−RV indicators before sizing. No rolls or early closes were required during the period.
- **Layer 3 (episodic/tactical).** No Layer 3 activity this month. One watchlist name reached the pre-earnings window but failed the positioning filter; the trade was passed.

## Key observations

- The operational discipline of documenting entry rationale at the point of trade — rather than reconstructing it later — is already paying dividends in review clarity. The monthly report practically writes itself when the log is kept current.
- The psychological guardrail of not reacting to mark-to-market pain was not meaningfully tested this month, given the benign regime. That test is coming.
- Margin utilization stayed comfortably within the 28–52% target band. The Notional Cap was never close to engaged.

## Forward stance

Posture into June is unchanged: contango regime, Layer 2 active within policy bands, Layer 1 held, Layer 3 opportunistic. One macro catalyst of note on the June calendar; trade will be sized to respect path risk rather than point estimate.

The first twelve months are, by design, about establishing the track record and proving that the framework holds under ordinary conditions. The interesting months will be the ones where it does not.
